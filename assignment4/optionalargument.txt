<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa" class="mp-contains-float"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 154px;">
<span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:Modus_ponendo_ponens.png" class="mw-file-description" title="Modus ponens, one of the valid forms of inference studied in logic"><img alt="Modus ponens, one of the valid forms of inference studied in logic" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Modus_ponendo_ponens.png/154px-Modus_ponendo_ponens.png" decoding="async" width="154" height="127" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Modus_ponendo_ponens.png/231px-Modus_ponendo_ponens.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Modus_ponendo_ponens.png/308px-Modus_ponendo_ponens.png 2x" data-file-width="682" data-file-height="562" /></a></span><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;"><i><a href="/wiki/Modus_ponens" title="Modus ponens">Modus ponens</a></i>, one of the valid forms of inference studied in logic</div></div>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Logic" title="Logic">Logic</a></b> is the study of <a href="/wiki/Logical_reasoning" title="Logical reasoning">correct reasoning</a>. Formal logic aims to determine the correctness of arguments based on their structure alone and often uses a <a href="/wiki/Formal_language" title="Formal language">formal language</a> to analyze them. <a href="/wiki/Informal_logic" title="Informal logic">Informal logic</a> examines arguments expressed in <a href="/wiki/Natural_language" title="Natural language">natural language</a> and also takes their content and context into account. Logic distinguishes between <a href="/wiki/Deductive_reasoning" title="Deductive reasoning">deductive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inductive_reasoning" title="Inductive reasoning">inductive</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Abductive_reasoning" title="Abductive reasoning">abductive arguments</a>. Deductive arguments have the strongest form of support: if their premises are true then their conclusion must also be true, such as in the argument "today is Sunday; if today is Sunday then I do not have to work today; therefore I do not have to work today". Inductive arguments are generalizations, like inferring that all ravens are black based on many individual observations. Abductive arguments are <a href="/wiki/Inference" title="Inference">inferences</a> to the best explanation, for instance, when a doctor concludes that a patient has a certain disease that explains the symptoms they suffer. Arguments that fall short of the standards of reasoning embody <a href="/wiki/Fallacy" title="Fallacy">fallacies</a>. (<b><a href="/wiki/Logic" title="Logic">Full&#160;article...</a></b>)
</p>

<td style="padding:0 6px 0 0">
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Pontia_edusa" title="Pontia edusa">Pontia edusa</a></b></i>, also known as the eastern Bath white, is a butterfly in the family <a href="/wiki/Pieridae" title="Pieridae">Pieridae</a>. It is found from southeastern Europe (southern France, Italy, Corsica, Sardinia) up to central Europe and the Middle East in Iran and Iraq. It is a migrant which can also be encountered in Belgium, Holland, northern Germany and Poland, in the Baltic states and in southern Sweden and Norway. It resides in open grassy or flowery areas, in stony or rocky places and in roadsides, especially where the host plants grow, at an altitude of up to 2,300 metres (7,500&#160;ft). This <i>P.&#160;edusa</i> butterfly was photographed near the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe</a> in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%BChst%C3%A4dt" title="Rühstädt">Rühstädt</a>, Germany.
